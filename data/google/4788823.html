A closed loop solar collector system (10) includes a fluid receiver (12) for collecting solar heat to vaporize a working fluid therein. A uniflow engine (15), connected to the receiver (12), is powered by the vaporized fluid to power a water wheel (19). The engine (15) includes a single piston (37) acting directly upon a pair of normally closed intake valves (50) projecting into the engine cylinder (35). Under low boiler pressure conditions, a spring loaded connecting rod (62) having depending arms (64) each engaging one of the intake valve rods (54b) when in a normally raised position coordinates simultaneous opening or closing of the valves (50) with a detent mechanism (63), enabling the piston (37) to operate in a nonexpanding state. Claims

1. A valve mechanism, suitable for controlling the flow of a working fluid supplied from a fluid source under a variable source pressure to operate an engine with a piston reciprocating in a cylinder, comprising:

means for connecting said valve mechanism to said fluid source; and
inlet valve means operable in a first mode for admitting said working fluid into said cylinder at a first essentially constant inlet pressure during substantially an entire working stroke of said piston when said variable source pressure is lower than a predetermined reference pressure and operable in a second mode for admitting said working fluid at a second essentially constant inlet pressure only during a starting portion of a working stroke so that the admitted fluid thereafter expands during the balance of the corresponding working stroke when said variable source pressure is higher than said predetermined reference pressure.

2. The valve mechanism according to claim 1, further comprising:

mode-changing means responsive to said variable source pressure for causing said inlet valve means to change operation of said inlet valve means between said first and second modes of operation thereof.

3. The valve mechanism according to claim 1, wherein:

said first essentially constant inlet pressure and said second essentially constant inlet pressure are each substantially equal to the respective instantaneous values of the corresponding variable source pressure.

4. The valve mechanism according to claim 1, wherein:

said mode-changing means comprises rotating means operable by an output of said engine for rotating at a speed related to said variable source pressure, whereby the change of operation of said valve means between said first and second modes is relatable to a speed of operation of said engine.

5. A valve mechanism, suitable for controlling the flow of a working fluid supplied from a fluid source at a pressure sufficient to operate an engine with a piston reciprocating in a cylinder to generate a rotational output, comprising:

inlet valve means operable in a first mode for admitting said working fluid into said cylinder at an essentially constant inlet pressure during substantially an entire working stroke of said piston and operable in a second mode for admitting said working fluid at said essentially constant inlet pressure only during a starting portion of a working stroke so that the fluid thus admitted into the cylinder thereafter expands during the balance of the corresponding working stroke; and
mode-changing means responsive to a selected operating parameter of said engine for causing said inlet valve means to change operation of said inlet valve means between said first and second modes of operation thereof.

6. The valve mechanism according to claim 5, wherein:

the selected operating parameter is a rotational speed of the engine.

7. A method for controlling the flow of a working fluid supplied from a fluid source at a pressure sufficient to operate an engine with a piston reciprocating in a cylinder to generate a rotational output, comprising the steps of:

providing said working fluid to said cylinder to generate a working stroke in a first mode when an operating parameter of said engine is at less than a predetermined reference value thereof, said first mode being characterized in that the inflow of working fluid takes place at an essentially constant inlet pressure during substantially the entire extent of said working stroke of said piston; and
providing said working fluid to said cylinder to generate a working stroke in a second mode when said operating parameter of said engine exceeds said reference value, said second mode being characterized in that the inflow of working fluid takes places at said essentially constant inlet pressure only during an initial portion of said working stroke, whereafter said inflow is terminated and the quantity of working fluid thus admitted into the cylinder expands during substantially the balance of said working stroke of said piston.

8. The flow control method according to claim 7, wherein:

said operating parameter of said engine is a rotational speed thereof.
