A protection scheme to protect pulse width modulated drives is described. The scheme is implantable in both hardware and software and combinations thereof. The semiconductor devices of the drive are protected from transient signals such as power line spikes and loss of line. The present scheme uses an adaptive technique to determine the normal or steady state distortion (transients and harmonics) value in an unfiltered power signal. The present distortion value is compared to the normal distortion. If the present distortion exceeds the steady state value by a given amount, then the drive is placed in freewheel mode to protect the semiconductor devices in the drive.

Claims

1. A pulse width modulated, adjustable speed drive, comprising:

an input filter connected to a power source;

a rectifier connected to the input filter;

an inverter connected to a motor;

a dc link connecting the rectifier to the inverter; and
a controller operably connected to the rectifier and the inverter, the controller including a drive protection unit connected to the power source and to sense transient events from the power source by converting a power signal to a vector representation and filtering the vector representation, the drive protection unit to place the rectifier and the inverter in protection mode when a transient event occurs.

2. The drive of claim 1, wherein the protection mode includes both the rectifier and the inverter in a freewheel mode.

3. The drive of claim 2, wherein the drive protection unit always places the rectifier in freewheel mode before the inverter.

4. The drive of claim 3, wherein the drive protection unit to return the rectifier and the inverter to a normal operation mode after power from the transient event has dissipated.

5. A pulse width modulated, adjustable speed drive comprising:

an input filter connected to a power source;

a rectifier connected to the input filter;

an inverter connected to a motor;

a dc link connecting the rectifier to the inverter;
a controller operably connected to the rectifier and the inverter, the controller including a drive protection unit connected to the power source and to sense transient events from the power source, the drive protection unit being to place the rectifier and the inverter in protection mode when a transient event occurs;
wherein the protection mode includes both the rectifier and the inverter in a freewheel mode;
wherein the drive protection unit always places the rectifier in freewheel mode before the inverter;
wherein the drive protection unit is to return the rectifier and the inverter to a normal operation mode after power from the transient event has dissipated;
wherein the drive protection unit returns the rectifier to normal operation before the inverter is in a regeneration mode.

6. The drive of claim 5, wherein the drive protection unit returns the inverter to normal operation before the rectifier in a motoring mode.

7. The drive of claim 1, wherein the rectifier includes a plurality of symmetrical gate commutated thyristors.

8. The drive of claim 7, wherein the inverter includes a plurality of symmetrical gate commutated thyristors.

9. The drive of claim 8, wherein the controller is operably connected to the symmetrical gate commutated thyristors of both the rectifier and the inverter to control conduction state of the thyristors.

10. The drive of claim 1, wherein the protection mode includes the rectifier in a freewheel mode and the inverter in a phase-shifted, pulse-width-modulated mode.

11. A pulse width modulated, adjustable speed drive, comprising:

an input filter connected to a power source;

a rectifier connected to the input filter;

an inverter connected to a motor;

a dc link connecting the rectifier to the inverter; and
a controller operably connected to the rectifier and the inverter, the controller including a drive protection unit connected to the power source, the drive protection unit to sense transient events from the power source, the drive protection unit to place the rectifier and the inverter in protection, freewheeling mode when a transient event occurs, the drive protection unit to return the rectifier to normal operation before the inverter is in a regeneration mode.

12. The drive of claim 11, wherein the protection mode includes both the rectifier and the inverter in a freewheel mode.

13. The drive of claim 11, wherein the drive protection unit always places the rectifier in a freewheel mode before the inverter is placed in a freewheel mode.

14. The drive of claim 11, wherein the drive protection unit is adapted to return the rectifier and the inverter to a normal operation mode after power from the transient event has dissipated.

15. The drive of claim 11, wherein the protection mode includes the rectifier in a freewheel mode and the inverter in a phase-shifted, pulse-width-modulated mode.

16. The drive of claim 11, wherein the drive protection unit is adapted to sample the power signal at at least 4 kHz.

17. The drive of claim 5, wherein the drive protection unit is to sample a power signal at at least 4 kHz.

18. The drive of claim 1, wherein the drive protection unit includes a vector rotator to transfer a monitored power signal into the vector representation.

19. The drive of claim 18, wherein the drive protection unit includes a high pass filter to remove a fundamental component of the power signal such that a nonfundamental component passes the filter.

20. The drive of claim 19, wherein the drive protection unit includes a rectifier adapted to rectify the nonfundamental component.

21. The drive of claim 20, wherein the drive protection unit includes a peak detector to determine a steady state amount of rectified nonfundamental component.

22. The drive of claim 21, wherein the drive protection unit includes a low pass filter adapted to output a steady state nonfundamental signal.

23. The drive of claim 22, wherein the drive protection unit includes a multiplier to scale the steady state nonfundamental signal.

24. The drive of claim 23, wherein the drive protection unit is to correct the steady state nonfundamental signal for current demand by motor receiving the output signal.

25. The drive of claim 24, wherein the circuit protector is to compare the correct steady state nonfundamental signal to the rectified nonfundamental component.

26. The drive of claim 25, wherein the drive protection unit is to detect nonfundamentals on a medium voltage utility line.

27. The drive of claim 26, wherein the drive protection unit is to sample the power signal at at least 4 kHz.
