This invention relates to a VSCF system for aircraft power generation having a DC source, the DC source producing a substantially constant voltage DC signal. An inverter is coupled to the DC source and receives the DC signal, applying a PWM pattern of switching transients to the DC signal to produce an AC signal from the DC signal. The subject invention contemplates the addition of a feedback circuit which comprises a transformer providing an inductive coupling between the AC signal at a point of regulation and the DC signal at a point between the DC source and the inverter to eliminate short duration switching transients in the PWM pattern. Modulation of the DC signal allows short duration switching transients in the PWM pattern to be lengthened, thereby avoiding physical minimum time switching limitations in the switches of the inverter. Claims

1. An AC power source, comprising:

converter means for receiving a DC signal and applying a PWM pattern of switching transients to said DC signal to produce an AC signal from said DC signal; and
feedback means for receiving said AC signal, said feedback means modulating said DC signal to eliminate short duration switching transients in said PWM pattern.

2. The AC source as recited in claim 1 wherein said DC signal is produced by means for producing a wild frequency AC signal coupled to means for rectifying said wild frequency AC signal into said DC signal.

3. The AC power source as recited in claim 2 wherein said means for applying a PWM pattern comprises:

(a) inverter means responsive to said DC signal and an inverter control output signal to provide a controlled inverter output pattern signal; and
(b) inverter control means to provide said inverter control output signal to said inverter means.

4. The AC power source as recited in claim 3 wherein said feedback means comprises a transformer which provides an inductive coupling between said AC signal at a point of regulation and said DC signal at a point between said means for rectifying and said inverter means.

5. The AC power source as recited in claim 4 wherein said AC signal is provided to electrical loads.

6. The AC power source as recited in claim 5 wherein said inverter means is controlled in response to said AC signal.

7. The AC power source is recited in claim 6 wherein said feedback means is coupled to said AC signal via a transformer.

8. In a VSCF system having a DC source, said DC source producing a substantially constant voltage DC signal and inverter means for receiving said DC signal and applying a PWM pattern of switching transients to said DC signal to produce an AC signal from said DC signal, and AC feedback modulator, comprising:

feedback means for receiving said AC signal, said feedback means modulating said DC signal to eliminate short duration switching transients in said PWM pattern.

9. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 8 wherein said feedback means provides an inductive coupling between said AC signal at a point of regulation and said DC signal at a point between means for rectifying and said inverter means.

10. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 9 wherein said inverter means is responsive to an inverter control output signal to provide said AC signal.

11. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 10 wherein said DC source comprises means to provide a wild frequency AC signal and said means to rectify said wild frequency AC signal into said DC signal.

12. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 11 wherein said inverter control means comprises means responsive to said AC signal to provide said inverter control output signal to said inverter means.

13. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 12 wherein said AC signal is filtered to produce a filtered AC signal.

14. The AC feedback modulator as recited in claim 13 wherein said filtered AC signal is provided to said point of regulation.

15. A method for providing AC power, comprising the steps of:

receiving a DC signal;
applying a PWM pattern of switching transients to said DC signal to produce an AC signal from said DC signal from said DC signal;
receiving said AC signal into a feedback means; and
modulating said DC signal with said feedback means to eliminate short duration switching transients in said PWM pattern.

16. The method as recited in claim 15 wherein said DC signal is provided by means for producing a wild frequency AC signal coupled to means for rectifying said wild frequency AC signal into said DC signal.

17. The method as recited in claim 16 wherein inverter control means provides an inverter control output signal to inverter means to produce said AC signal from said DC signal.

18. The method as recited in claim 17 wherein said feedback means varies a voltage of said DC signal to eliminate said short duration switching transients in said PWM pattern.

19. The method as recited in claim 18 wherein said feedback means provides an inductive coupling between said AC signal at a point of regulation and said DC signal at a point between said means for rectifying and said inverter means.

20. The method as recited in claim 19 wherein said AC signal is filtered and provided to electrical loads.
