A retractor and a surgical tool are positioned within a cannula, and a dissection cradle of the retractor is positioned at the distal end of the cannula. The retractor includes a first portion with an axis approximately parallel to the axis of the cannula and a second portion with an axis skewed relative to the axis of the cannula. The dissection cradle is located at the distal end of the second portion of the retractor, and may include two substantially parallel, spaced legs with the retractor shaped in a loop between and in a plane skewed relative to the axes of the legs, and with the loop directed away from the surgical tool. Thus, in operation, when the surgeon locates a vessel and side branch of interest, the surgeon extends the retractor to cradle the vessel in the dissection cradle. Once cradled, the retractor may be fully extended to urge the vessel away from the axis of the cannula to isolate the side branch for exposure to the surgical tool. Claims

1. A surgical apparatus comprising:

an elongated cannula having an instrument lumen for supporting a surgical instrument and an endoscopic lumen for supporting an endoscope, the endoscopic lumen having a central axis eccentric to a central axis of the cannula;
a transparent tip positioned at a distal end of the cannula, having tapered walls converging toward a distal end that is substantially aligned with the central axis of the endoscopic lumen for providing visualization through the transparent tip via an endoscope within the endoscopic lumen;
the tip including a mounting rod attached to a proximal end of the tip and including a mounting structure disposed at a proximal end of the mounting rod to engage the proximal end of the cannula for securing the tip at the distal end of the cannula.

2. The surgical apparatus of claim 1 wherein the tip further comprises:

a cylindrical proximal portion; and
a conical end portion, wherein the conical end portion has unequal taper angles relative to a plane of transition between the cylindrical and conical portions of the tip, for aligning the distal end of the conical portion with the central axis of the endoscopic lumen.

3. The surgical apparatus of claim 1, wherein the tip further comprises:

a conical portion, positioned at a distal end of the tip; and
a mounting rod, attached to a proximal end of the conical portion, having a locking section disposed at a proximal end of the mounting rod.

4. The surgical apparatus according to claim 1 in which the mounting rod is flexible and extends through a lumen in the cannula that is skewed relative to the endoscopic lumen near a proximal end thereof.

5. The surgical apparatus of claim 1 in which the tip further comprises:

a proximal portion for attachment to a distal end of the cannula; and
a distal portion including the distal end disposed forward of the proximal portion to form a transition plane between the proximal and distal portions skewed from the normal to central axis of the cannula for positioning the distal end of the distal portion in substantial alignment with the central axis of the endoscopic lumen.

6. The surgical apparatus of claim 5, wherein the proximal portion of the tip further comprises:

a cylindrical tube, having an upper region and a lower region, in which a distal end of the upper region extends beyond a distal edge of the lower region; and the distal portion of the tip further comprises:
a conical end, having an upper and lower region, in which a proximal end of the lower region extends proximally beyond a proximal edge of the upper region.

7. The surgical apparatus of claim 5 wherein taper angles formed relative to the plane of transition between the cylindrical tube and the conical end are substantially equal.

8. A surgical apparatus comprising:

an elongated cannula having an endoscopic lumen for supporting an endoscope, the endoscopic lumen having a central axis eccentric to a central axis of the cannula;
a transparent tip positioned at a distal end of the cannula, having tapered walls converging toward a distal end that is substantially aligned with the central axis of the endoscopic lumen, for providing visualization through the transparent tip via an endoscope within the endoscopic lumen;
the tip including a mounting rod attached to a proximal end of the tip and including a mounting structure disposed at a proximal end of the mounting rod; and
the cannula comprises:
a mating structure, disposed near a proximal end of the cannula for mating with the mounting structure near the proximal end of the mounting rod to retain the tip on the distal end of the cannula.

9. The surgical apparatus according to claim 8 in which the mounting structure of the mounting rod includes threads thereon for mating engagement with the mating structure disposed near the proximal end of the cannula.

10. An apparatus for dilating tissue, comprising:

an elongated cannula, having a distal and proximal ends; and
a tapered tip, attached to the distal end of the elongated cannula, comprising:
a pair of protrusions extending laterally from the tip, each protrusion positioned on opposite sides of the tip.

11. The apparatus of claim 10, wherein the pair of protrusions have distal ends disposed near the distal end of the tip.

12. The apparatus of claim 10, wherein the pair of protrusions are in a planar configuration and are tapered towards the distal end of the cannula.

13. The apparatus of claim 12, wherein the pair of protrusions are in a planar configuration and curved in a parabolic configuration.

14. A method of dissecting and transecting vessels using a cannula having a selectively displaceable tip disposed on the distal end of the cannula, the tip having an axis aligned with a central axis of an endoscopic lumen which is eccentric to a central axis of the cannula, comprising the steps of:

incising skin to expose an area near a target vessel;
bluntly dissecting the incision to expose the target vessel;
sealing the incision;
insufflating a tunnel along the target vessel;
advancing the cannula through tissue along the target vessel under endoscopic visualization through the offset tip to form a tunnel in tissue adjacent the target vessel;
removing the cannula from the tunnel;
removing the offset tip from the distal end of the cannula;
inserting the cannula into the tunnel through the incision; and
transecting side branches and vessel ends under endoscopic visualization through use of endoscopic instruments in cannula.

15. The method of claim 14 performed using a transparent dilating tip having a pair of protrusions bilaterally extending therefrom, the method further comprising the steps of:

substituting the dilating tip for the offset tip;
advancing the cannula under endoscopic visualization through the dilating tip along the insufflated tunnel to dissect tissue surrounding the tunnel in response to advancement of the cannula and dilating tip therethrough;
removing the cannula and dilating tip from the tunnel; and
removing the dilating tip.

16. A method of dissecting and transecting vessels using a cannula having a selectively displaceable tip disposed on the distal end of the cannula, the tip having an axis aligned with a central axis of an endoscopic lumen which is eccentric to a central axis of the cannula, the method comprising the steps of:

incising skin to expose an area near a target vessel;
bluntly dissecting the incision to expose the target vessel;
advancing the cannula through tissue along the target vessel under endoscopic visualization through the offset tip to form a tunnel in tissue adjacent the target vessel;
removing the cannula from the tunnel;
removing the offset tip from the distal end of the cannula;
inserting the cannula into the tunnel through the incision; and
transecting side branches and vessel ends under endoscopic visualization using endoscopic instruments extended through the cannula distally of the distal end thereof.

17. The method according to claim 16 including:

sealing the incision; and
insufflating a tunnel along the target vessel.

18. A method of dissecting and transecting vessels using a cannula having a selectively displaceable transparent tip disposed on the distal end of the cannula, comprising the steps of:

incising skin to expose an area near a target vessel;
bluntly dissecting the incision to expose the target vessel;
sealing the incision;
insufflating a tunnel along the target vessel; and
advancing the cannula through tissue along the target vessel under endoscopic visualization through the transparent tip to form a tunnel in tissue adjacent the target vessel;
removing the cannula from the tunnel;
removing the tip from the distal end of the cannula;
inserting the cannula into the tunnel through the incision; and
transecting side branches and vessel ends under endoscopic visualization through use of endoscopic instruments in the cannula.
