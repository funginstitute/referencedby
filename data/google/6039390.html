Chromogenic window assembly construction and other chromogenic devices

 Anoop Agrawal et al
› Overview

Patent number: 6039390
Filing date: Aug 20, 1997
Issue date: Mar 21, 2000

A chromogenic window panel assembly, and a chromogenic skylight each includes an outer panel and a chromogenic member having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal. A chromogenic light pipe includes a light harvesting member and a chromogenic member having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal. A chromogenic vehicle sunroof, a chromogenic aircraft window assembly, and a chromogenic window assembly each includes a panel having a particular panel contour corresponding to a surface contour and a chromogenic panel having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal. A chromogenic block includes two panels and a chromogenic panel having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal.

Inventors: Anoop Agrawal, Juan Carlos Lopez Tonazzi, Robert Steven LeCompte, Carl Baertelin, Douglas Alan Fischer
Original Assignee: Donnelly Corporation

Claims

1. A chromogenic window panel assembly to be installed in a surface having a surface contour and subject to exposure to a solar radiation source, comprising:

a chromogenic member having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal; and
a light-transmissive outer panel substantially spaced from said chromogenic member, said outer panel being dimensioned and disposed so as to overlap at least a substantial part of the chromogenic member, and having a non-flat surface substantially following the surface contour, wherein said outer panel is disposed between said solar radiation source and said chromogenic member when said window panel assembly is installed in said surface, wherein said chromogenic member comprises a flat, electrochromic member.

2. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, wherein said chromogenic member is selected from the group consisting of electrochromic devices, photochromic devices, liquid crystal devices, user-controllable-photochromic devices, polymer-dispersed-liquid-crystal devices, suspended particle devices, thermochromic devices, and thermotropic devices.

3. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, further comprising a layer of a material disposed upon at least one of said chromogenic member and said outer panel.

4. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 3, wherein said layer of said material changes an optical property of said chromogenic window panel assembly.

5. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 3, wherein said layer of said material changes a physical property of said chromogenic window panel assembly selected from the group consisting of surface hardness, scratch resistance and shatter prevention.

6. A chromogenic window panel assembly according to claim 1, wherein said assembly comprises one of a vehicle sunroof, aircraft window, building skylight, building light pipe, and architectural block.

7. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, wherein said chromogenic member comprises two separate, flat electrochromic panels.

8. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, further comprising a thermal break.

9. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 8, wherein said thermal break comprises an air volume.

10. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, wherein said surface is a roof of a vehicle.

11. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, further comprising:

at least one of
a first sensor positioned proximate to said outer panel so as to measure an amount of radiation which has come from outside and passed through said outer panel, said first sensor outputting a first signal in response thereto, and
a second sensor positioned proximate to said chromogenic member so as to measure an amount of radiation which has first passed through said outer panel and then passed through said chromogenic member, said second sensor outputting a second signal in response thereto.

12. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 1, further comprising a driver for supplying the applied signal to the chromogenic member.

13. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 12, further comprising a controller which generates the applied signal applied by said driver to the chromogenic material so as to regulate the optical property of the chromogenic member.

14. A chromogenic window panel assembly as in claim 13, wherein the controller receives at least one of the first signal output from the first sensor and the second signal from the second sensor and generates the applied signal in accordance therewith.

15. A chromogenic vehicle sunroof to be installed in a vehicle roof and subject to exposure to a solar radiation source, said roof having a surface contour, comprising:

a chromogenic member having an optical property which varies according to an applied signal; and
a light-transmissive outer panel substantially spaced from said chromogenic member, said outer panel being dimensioned and disposed so as to overlap at least a substantial part of the chromogenic member, said outer panel having a predetermined non-flat surface substantially following the surface contour, wherein said outer panel is disposed between said solar radiation source and said chromogenic member when said vehicle sunroof is installed in said vehicle roof, wherein said chromogenic member comprises a flat, electrochromic member.

16. A chromogenic vehicle sunroof according to claim 15, further comprising:

a seal,

17. 18. A chromogenic vehicle sunroof as in claim 15, wherein said chromogenic member is selected from the group consisting of electrochromic devices, photochromic devices, liquid crystal devices, user-controllable-photochromic devices, polymer-dispersed-liquid-crystal devices, suspended particle devices, thermochromic devices, and thermotropic devices.
