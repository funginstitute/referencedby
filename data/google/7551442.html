Embedded heat pipe in a hybrid cooling system

Application number: 11/294,825

One embodiment of a system for cooling a heat-generating device includes a base adapted to be coupled to the heat-generating device, a housing coupled to the base, a liquid channel formed between the base and the housing, where a heat transfer liquid may be circulated through the liquid channel to remove heat generated by the heat-generated device, and a heat pipe disposed within the liquid channel, where the heat pipe increases the heat transfer surface area to which the heat transfer liquid is exposed. Among other things, the heat pipe advantageously increases the heat transfer surface area to which the heat transfer liquid is exposed and efficiently spreads the heat generated by the heat-generating device over that heat transfer surface area. The result is enhanced heat transfer through the liquid channel relative to prior art cooling systems.

Claims

1. A system for cooling a heat-generating device, the system comprising:

a base adapted to be coupled to the heat-generating device;

a U-shaped housing coupled to the base, the heat generating device located over the base;

a U-shaped liquid channel formed between the base and the housing wherein the housing forms a portion of the liquid channel, wherein a heat transfer liquid is circulated through the liquid channel to remove heat generated by the heat-generated device; and

a U-shaped heat pipe disposed within the liquid channel, wherein the heat pipe increases the heat transfer surface area to which the heat transfer liquid is exposed, the heat pipe comprising a curved base portion adjacent the heat generating device and leg portions extending beyond the heat generating device to define cold heat pipe regions, the legs being coupled to a pump and a heat exchanger located at a distance from the heat generating device.

2. The system of claim 1 wherein the heat pipe includes a wick structure internal to the surface of the heat pipe for drawing liquid to the heat source through capillary attractors and a vapor chamber within the wick structure and central to the heat pipe for carrying heated vapor to the cold region of the heat pipe by a pressure gradient, the cooled liquid thereafter being returned to the region adjacent the heat generating device.

3. The system of claim 2, further comprising a plurality of fins coupled to a first side of the housing, wherein the housing has a second side forming the portion of the liquid channel.

4. The system of claim 3, wherein the plurality of fins and the housing form an integrated part.

5. The system of claim 3, further comprising a fan configured to force air through air channels defined between the fins.

6. The system of claim 2, wherein the heat pipe substantially extends the length of the fluid channel.

7. The system of claim 2, wherein an outer surface of the heat pipe is textured.

8. The system of claim 1, wherein the heat-generating device is a graphics processing unit.

9. The system of claim 8, wherein the graphics processing unit is disposed on a graphics card, and the system is configured to fit within one standard peripheral component interconnect slot.

10. The system of claim 1, wherein the heat-generating device is a central processing unit, an application-specific integrated circuit or a memory device.

11. A computer system configured with a cooling system, the computer system comprising:

a heat-generating device, wherein the cooling system includes:
a base coupled to the heat-generating device,
a U-shaped housing coupled to the base, the heat generating device located over the base,
a U-shaped liquid channel formed between the base and the housing housing forms a portion of the liquid channel, wherein a heat transfer liquid is circulated through the liquid channel to remove heat generated by the heat-generated device, and
a U-shaped heat pipe disposed within the liquid channel, wherein the heat pipe increases the heat transfer surface area to which the heat transfer liquid is exposed, the heat pipe comprising a curved base portion adjacent the heat generating device and leg portions extending beyond the heat generating device to define cold heat pipe regions, the leas being coupled to a pump and a heat exchanger located at a distance from the heat generating device.

12. The system of claim 11 wherein the heat pipe includes a wick structure internal to the surface of the heat pipe for drawing liquid to the heat source through capillary attractors and a vapor chamber within the wick structure and central to the heat pipe for carrying heated vapor to the cold region of the heat pipe by a pressure gradient, the cooled liquid thereafter being returned to the region adjacent the heat generating device.

13. The computer system of claim 12, further comprising a plurality of fins coupled to a first side of the housing, wherein the housing has a second side forming the portion of the liquid channel.

14. The computer system of claim 13, wherein the plurality of fins and the housing form an integrated part.

15. The computer system of claim 13, further comprising a fan configured to force air through air channels defined between the fins.

16. The computer system of claim 12, wherein the heat pipe substantially extends the length of the fluid channel.

17. The computer system of claim 12, wherein the heat pipe is coupled to the housing and the base such that the heat pipe deforms from a circular cross-section to an oval-shape cross-section.

18. The computer system of claim 12, wherein an outer surface of the heat pipe is textured.

19. The computer system of claim 12, further including a pump configured to circulate the heat transfer liquid through the liquid channel, and a heat exchanger configured to receive the heat transfer liquid circulated through the liquid channel and to transfer heat from the heat transfer liquid to a local environment.
