A process for obtaining a sample from an atmosphere in a closed gastight vessel, preferably from a reactor safety vessel of a nuclear power station, includes passing a sample through a venturi nozzle immediately upon entry of the sample into a sample-taking container in a vessel. The sample is mixed in the venturi nozzle with a transport fluid serving as a washing liquid. Gaseous constituents of the sample being soluble and/or condensable in the washing liquid are subsequently discharged together with the washing liquid from the sample-taking container and from the vessel by triggering a pressure reduction. A device for obtaining samples from an atmosphere in a closed gastight vessel, preferably from a reactor safety vessel of a nuclear power station, includes a sample-taking container having a bottom and a given volume. Claims

1. In combination with a closed gastight vessel, a device for obtaining samples from an atmosphere in the vessel, comprising:

a sample-taking container having a bottom and a given volume;
a washing liquid being disposed in said sample-taking container and having a volume being at most approximately equal to half of said given volume;
a venturi nozzle dipping into said washing liquid in said sample-taking container above said bottom; and
an inlet channel leading into said sample-taking container below said venturi nozzle.

2. The device according to claim 1, wherein said inlet channel serves for the admission of samples and has a free end and a predetermined volume between said free end and said bottom, and said volume of said washing liquid is slightly greater than said predetermined volume.

3. The device according to claim 1, wherein said venturi nozzle is formed of filling bodies serving as flow distributors and a plurality of nozzles in said bottom of said sample-taking container.

4. The device according to claim 1, wherein said inlet channel has a free end, an inlet opening at said free end and a bursting disk closing said inlet opening during normal use of the vessel.

5. The device according to claim 1, wherein said sample-taking container has a dome, and including a filling and emptying line at said bottom for said washing liquid, and a gas line connected to said dome.

6. The device according to claim 5, wherein said sample-taking container has a side, said filling and emptying line is guided upwards at said side, and including an injector connecting said filling and emptying line to said gas line laterally above said dome.

7. The device according to claim 1, wherein said sample-taking container is disposed inside the vessel.

8. The device according to claim 7, wherein the vessel is a reactor safety vessel.

9. The device according to claim 6, including in a line starting from said injector and passing to the outside through an outer wall of the vessel, and a throttle in said line for limiting a flow through said line.

10. The device according to claim 9, including a sorption filter for organoiodine being inserted into said line.

11. The device according to claim 1, including polished or teflon-coated surfaces being in contact with the sample.

12. The device according to claim 1, wherein said inlet channel has polished or teflon-coated surfaces being in contact with the sample.

13. The device according to claim 9, wherein said sample-taking container has built-in fittings, and said inlet channel, said sample-taking container with all of said built-in fittings, said filling and emptying line, said gas line, said injector and said line starting from said injector are formed essentially of metal.

14. The device according to claim 13, wherein said metal is steel.

15. The device according to claim 1, wherein said volume of said washing liquid filling said sample-taking container is substantially between 2 and 3 liters.

16. The device according to claim 1, including means for maintaining a constant velocity of flow of the sample by throttling in said venturi nozzle.

17. The device according to claim 1, including means for maintaining a constant velocity of flow of the sample by throttling with a throttle disposed outside the vessel.

18. The device according to claim 1, including means for maintaining a constant velocity of flow of the sample by throttling in said venturi nozzle and with a throttle disposed outside the vessel.
