The present invention provides improved, low-cost fuel cells having reduced fuel crossover, reduced sensitivity to metal ion impurities and ability to operate under a broad range of temperatures. Additionally, new effective organic fuels are described for use in such fuel cells. The invention further provides improved methods for catalyst preparation and a new integrated flow field system for use in H2/O2 fuel cells.

Claims

1. A liquid feed direct oxidation fuel cell having an anode, a cathode, a proton conducting membrane disposed between said anode and said cathode, means for supplying an organic fuel, mixture of fuels or organic fuel aqueous solution to the anode and means for supplying oxygen to the cathode, wherein said organic fuel is selected from a group consisting of oxalic acid, dimethyloxalat, glycerol and ethylene glycol and their oxalic and formic esters, glyoxylic acid and its methyl ester, and glyoxylic aldehyde.

2. A hybrid power source comprising a liquid feed fuel cell according to claim 1, a DC to DC converter and a rechargeable battery.

3. A hybrid power source comprising:

a) a fuel cell which comprises a housing, a solid electrolyte membrane having a first and second surface, disposed in said housing to partition it into an anode side and a cathode side, an anode and a cathode each comprising a catalyst layer and a carbon backing layer and being formed on said first and second membrane surfaces respectively, so as to connect said first surface to the anode side and said second surface to the cathode side, said anode side comprising means for storing an oxidizable fuel or for circulating it past the anode and said cathode side comprising means for flowing oxygen or air past the cathode, wherein said solid electrolyte membrane is a proton conducting membrane having pores with a diameter size which is essentially smaller than 30 nm and comprising:
(i) 5% to 60% by volume of an electrically nonconductive inorganic powder having a good acid absorption capacity, said powder comprising essentially nanosize particles;
(ii) 5% to 50% by volume of a polymeric binder that is chemically compatible with acid, oxygen and said fuel; and
(iii) 10% to 90% by volume of an acid or aqueous acid solution;
(b) a DC to DC converter; and
(c) a rechargeable battery.

4. A hybrid power source according to claim 2, wherein the number of cells in said fuel cell is two or three and said rechargeable battery is a lithium ion battery.

5. A fuel cell according to claim 1 where the CO2 produced during the operation thereof is released through a thin hydrophobic porous matrix placed in the anode compartment or in the fuel tank.

6. A fuel cell which comprises a housing, a solid electrolyte membrane having a first and second surface, disposed in said housing to partition it into an anode side and a cathode side, an anode and a cathode each comprising a catalyst layer and a carbon backing layer and being formed on said first and second membrane surfaces respectively, so as to connect said first surface to the anode side and said second surface to the cathode side, said arnode side comprising means for storing an oxidizable fuel or for circulating it past the anode and said cathode side comprising means for flowing oxygen or air past the cathode, wherein said solid electrolyte membrane is a proton conducting membrane having pores with a diameter size which is essentially smaller than 30 nm and comprising:

(i) 5% to 60% by volume of an electrically nonconductive inorganic powder having a good acid absorption capacity, said powder comprising essentially nanosize particles;
(ii) 5% to 50% by volume of a polymeric binder that is chemically compatible with acid, oxygen and said fuel; and
(iii) 10% to 90% by volume of an acid or aqueous acid solution;

where the CO2 produced during the operation thereof is released through a thin hydrophobic porous matrix placed in the anode compartment or in the fuel tank.

7. A device for controlling the water return flow from the cathode side to the anode side through a membrane in a fuel cell, comprising a water or fuel solution level sensor and air or oxygen pressure control unit which controls the air or oxygen gas pressure in the cathode chamber to increase as the level of water or fuel solution decreases.

8. A fuel cell according to claim 1, utilizing a device for controlling the water return flow from the cathode side to the anode side through a membrane in a fuel cell, comprising a water or fuel solution level sensor and air or oxygen pressure control unit which controls the air or oxygen gas pressure in the cathode chamber to increase as the level of water or fuel solution decreases.

9. A fuel cell which comprises a housing, a solid electrolyte membrane having a first and second surface, disposed in said housing to partition it into an anode side and a cathode side, an anode and a cathode each comprising a catalyst layer and a carbon backing layer and being formed on said first and second membrane surfaces respectively, so as to connect said first surface to the anode side and said second surface to the cathode side, said anode side comprising means for storing an oxidizable fuel or for circulating it past the anode and said cathode side comprising means for flowing oxygen or air past the cathode, wherein said solid electrolyte membrane is a proton conducting membrane having pores with a diameter size which is essentially smaller than 30 nm and comprising:

(i) 5% to 60% by volume of an electrically nonconductive inorganic powder having a good acid absorption capacity, said powder comprising essentially nanosize particles;
(ii) 5% to 50% by volume of a polymeric binder that is chemically compatible with acid, oxygen and said fuel; and
(iii) 10% to 90% by volume of an acid or aqueous acid solution;

utilizing a device for controlling the water return flow from the cathode side to the anode side in a fuel cell, comprising a water or fuel solution level sensor and air or oxygen pressure control unit placed in the cathode compartment.

10. A hybrid power source according to claim 3, wherein the number of cells in said fuel cell is two or three and said rechargeable battery is a lithium ion battery.

11. A fuel selected from the group consisting of oxalic acid, dimethyloxalat, glycerol and ethylene glycol and their oxalic and formic esters, glyoxylic acid and its methyl ester and glyoxylic aldehyde, for use in a direct oxidation fuel cell.
