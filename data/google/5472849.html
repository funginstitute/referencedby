Method for detecting inflammation

Patent number: 5472849
Filing date: Nov 24, 1993
Issue date: Dec 5, 1995

A method for the detection of the presence of inflammation in a patient by measuring the amount of circulating intercellular adhesion molecule (cICAM-1) in a sample of one or more bodily fluids of the patient and then comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample to standards normal for the bodily fluid or fluids assayed. The amount of cICAM-1 can be measured using anti-ICAM-1 antibodies. Higher than normal amounts of cICAM-1 indicate the presence of inflammation.

Claims

1. A method for detecting inflammation in a patient which comprises the steps of contacting a sample of one or more body fluids of a patient with an antibody which specifically binds to circulating intercellular adhesion molecule 1, cICAM-1, to determine the amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample, and comparing the amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample to the amount of cICAM-1 present in standard samples obtained form normal individuals, wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of inflammation in said patient.

2. A method for the detection of inflammation in a patient which comprises the steps of:

(a) contacting a sample of one or more bodily fluids of the patient, with an immobilized first antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1 and labelled second antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1;
(b) determining an amount of bound labelled second antibody as a measure of the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample;
(c) comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample with standards of cICAM-1 normal for the one or more bodily fluids assayed
wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of inflammation in said patient.

3. A method as recited in claim 2 wherein the first antibody and the labelled second antibody are monoclonal antibodies.

4. A method as recited in claim 3 wherein the first antibody is immobilized and the labelled second antibody is soluble.

5. A method for the detection of inflammation in a patient which comprises the steps of:

(a) contacting a sample of one or more bodily fluids of the patient with an immobilized first antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, the immobilized first antibody being insoluble in the sample, to form a first insoluble complex of cICAM-1 and the immobilized first antibody;
(b) incubating the first insoluble complex with a soluble labelled second antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, to form a final insoluble complex of the labelled second antibody, cICAM-1, and the immobilized first antibody;
(c) separating the final insoluble complex from the sample and any uncomplexed soluble labelled second antibody;
(d) determining either an amount of label associated with the final insoluble complex or an amount of uncomplexed label, as a measure of the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample;
(e) comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample with standards of cICAM-1 normal for the one or more bodily fluids
wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of inflammation in said patient.

6. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the immobilized first antibody is immobilized on a solid support.

7. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the one or more bodily fluids is serum.

8. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the one or more bodily fluids is bile.

9. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the one or more bodily fluids is amniotic fluid.

10. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the one or more bodily fluids is synovial fluid.

11. A method as recited in claim 5 wherein the one or more bodily fluids is plasma.

12. A method for the detection of rheumatoid arthritis in a patient suspected of suffering from rheumatoid arthritis, which comprises the steps of:

(a) contacting a sample of synovial fluid from the patient, with an immobilized first antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, the immobilized first antibody being insoluble in the sample, to form a first insoluble complex of cICAM-1 and the immobilized first antibody;
(b) contacting the first insoluble complex with a soluble labeled second antibody with specifically binds cICAM-1, to form a final insoluble complex of the labelled second antibody, cICAM-1 and the immobilized first antibody;
(c) separating the final insoluble complex from the sample and any uncomplexed soluble labelled second antibody;
(d) determining either an amount of label associated with the final insoluble complex or an amount of uncomplexed label, as a measure of the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample;
(e) comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample with standards of cICAM-1 normal for synovial fluid
wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of rheumatoid arthritis in said patient.

13. A method for the detection of inflammation in a patient which comprises the steps of:

(a) incubating a sample of one or more bodily fluids from the patient, with a soluble labelled second antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, to form a soluble complex of cICAM-1 and the labelled second antibody;
(b) incubating the soluble complex of (a) with an immobilized first antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, the immobilized first antibody being insoluble in the sample, to form a final insoluble complex of the labelled second antibody, cICAM-1 and the immobilized first antibody;
(c) separating the final insoluble complex from the sample and any uncomplexed soluble labelled second antibody;
(d) determining either an amount of label associated with the final insoluble complex or an amount of uncomplexed label, as a measure of the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample;
(e) comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample with standards of cICAM-1 normal for the one or more bodily fluids
wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of inflammation in said patient.

14. A method for the detection of inflammation in a patient which comprises the steps of:

(a) incubating a sample of one or more bodily fluids from the patient, with (i) an immobilized first antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, the immobilized first antibody being insoluble in the sample; and (ii) a soluble labelled second antibody which specifically binds cICAM-1, to form a final insoluble complex of the labelled second antibody, cICAM-1 and the immobilized first antibody;
(b) separating the final insoluble complex from the sample and any uncomplexed soluble labelled second antibody;
(c) determining either an amount of label associated with the final insoluble complex or an amount of uncomplexed label, as a measure of the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample;
(d) comparing the amount of cICAM-1 in the sample with standards of cICAM-1 normal for the one or more bodily fluids
wherein an elevated amount of cICAM-1 present in said sample is indicative of inflammation in said patient.
