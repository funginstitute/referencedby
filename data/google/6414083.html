A dispersion has particles dispersed in a continuous aqueous dispersion medium. The particles, preferably no more than about 2 micrometers in size, are formed of a mixture of styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene (S-EB-S) block copolymer having end blocks each with a weight average molecular weight of more than about 15,000 Daltons and an oil such as a mineral oil. The dispersion medium is a mixture of water and a surfactant. Articles are prepared by dipping a form into the dispersion and withdrawing the form to leave a film of the dispersion on the form. The water is evaporated from the film, leaving a coherent extensible film on the form.

Claims

1. An aqueous dispersion, comprising:

a dispersion medium comprising a mixture of water and a surfactant; and
a plurality of particles dispersed in the dispersion medium, each particle comprising a mixture of
a styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer, where the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer has end blocks each having a weight average molecular weight of more than about 15,000 Daltons, and
an oil.

2. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the surfactant is an anionic surfactant.

3. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the particles have an average size of no greater than about 2 micrometers.

4. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer comprises block copolymers of substantially the same molecular weight.

5. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer comprises a mixture of at least two styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymers of different molecular weights.

6. The aqueous dispersion of claim 5, wherein at least one of the S-EB-S block copolymer components has a total weight average molecular weight exceeding at least about 150,000 Daltons.

7. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the oil is a mineral oil.

8. The aqueous dispersion of claim 1, wherein the particles comprise a mixture of from about 30 to about 70 percent by weight oil, balance styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer.

9. An aqueous dispersion, comprising:

a dispersion medium comprising a mixture of water and a surfactant; and
a plurality of particles dispersed in the dispersion medium, each particle having an average size of no greater than about 2 micrometers and comprising a mixture of
a styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer having end blocks, each with a weight average molecular weight of more than about 15,000 Daltons, and
a mineral oil.

10. An aqueous dispersion particularly formulated for dip-forming elastomeric articles, comprising:

a dispersion medium comprising a mixture of water and a surfactant; and
a plurality of particles dispersed in the dispersion medium, each particle comprising a mixture of
a styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer, where the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer has end blocks each having a weight average molecular weight of more than about 15,000 Daltons, and
an oil,
and wherein said aqueous dispersion is configured to form a coherent extensible film when coated on a former and dried.

11. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the surfactant is an anionic surfactant.

12. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the particles have an average size of no greater than about 2 micrometers.

13. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer comprises block copolymers of substantially the same molecular weight.

14. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene, block copolymer comprises a mixture of at least two styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymers of different molecular weights.

15. The aqueous dispersion of claim 14, wherein at least one of the S-EB-S block copolymer components has a total weight average molecular weight exceeding at least about 150,000 Daltons.

16. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the oil is mineral oil.

17. The aqueous dispersion of claim 10, wherein the particles comprise a mixture of from about 30 to about 70 percent by weight oil, balance styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer.

18. An aqueous dispersion particularly formulated for dip-forming elastomeric articles, comprising:

a dispersion medium comprising a mixture of water and a surfactant; and
a plurality of particles dispersed in the dispersion medium, each particle having an average size of no greater than about 2 micrometers and comprising a mixture of
a styrene-ethylene/butylene-styrene block copolymer having end blocks, each with a weight average molecular weight of more than about 15,000 Daltons, and
a mineral oil,
and wherein said aqueous dispersion is configured to form a coherent extensible film when coated on a former and dried.
