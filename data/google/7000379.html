Application number: 10/454,059

A system and method for controlling an internal combustion engine for low emissions include an inner feedback control loop to control the engine fuel/air ratio with feedback provided by a first exhaust gas sensor and an outer feedback control loop that modifies the fuel/air ratio reference provided to the inner feedback control loop based on feedback signals provided by the first exhaust gas sensor and a second exhaust gas sensor. The fuel/air ratio reference signal controller adapts to the oxygen storage capacity of the catalyst by modeling the catalyst as an integrator with an unknown gain and estimating the catalyst gain based on the first and second exhaust gas sensor signals. Using the estimated catalyst gain, an adaptive controller gain factor is determined and subsequently used to determine the fuel/air ratio reference signal provided to the fuel/air ratio controller of the inner feedback control loop.

Claims

1. A method for controlling an internal combustion engine, the method comprising:

determining a catalyst gain based on signals from a first exhaust gas sensor positioned upstream relative to a catalyst and a second exhaust gas sensor positioned downstream relative to at least a portion of the catalyst by modeling the catalyst as an integrator with an unknown gain and estimating the catalyst gain;

determining a reference air/fuel ratio based on the catalyst gain; and

generating a fueling command such that an actual air/fuel ratio approaches the reference air/fuel ratio.

2. The method of claim 1 wherein the step of estimating the catalyst gain comprises:

estimating the catalyst gain using recursive least squares.

3. The method of claim 1 further comprising:

positioning the second exhaust gas sensor upstream relative to at least a portion of the catalyst.

4. The method of claim 1 further comprising:

determining an adaptive gain based on the catalyst gain, wherein the step of determining the reference air/fuel ratio includes determining the reference air/fuel ratio based on the adaptive gain.

5. The method of claim 4 further comprising

determining a difference between a signal from the second exhaust gas sensor and a reference signal;

selecting an air/fuel ratio offset based on the difference; and

applying the adaptive gain to the selected air/fuel ratio offset.

6. The method of claim 4 further comprising:

determining a difference between a signal from the second exhaust gas sensor and a reference signal;

selecting an air/fuel ratio offset based on the difference;

applying the adaptive gain to the offset to provide an adaptive offset;

integrating the difference between the signal from the second exhaust gas sensor and the reference signal; and
combining the difference after integrating with the adaptive offset to determine the reference air/fuel ratio.

7. The method of claim 6 wherein the step of generating a fueling command comprises:

generating a fueling command to reduce the difference between a signal from the first exhaust gas sensor and the reference air/fuel ratio.

8. A method for controlling an internal combustion engine, the method comprising:

controlling air/fuel ratio of the engine using an inner feedback control loop based on a reference air/fuel ratio and feedback from an universal exhaust gas oxygen sensor positioned upstream relative to a catalyst;

determining a catalyst gain based on the universal exhaust gas oxygen sensor and the heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor by modeling the catalyst as an integrator with an unknown gain and estimating the catalyst gain; and

generating the reference air/fuel ratio in an outer feedback control loop based on feedback from a heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor and a sensor reference voltage to reduce any error therebetween, the heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor being positioned downstream relative to a first portion of the catalyst and upstream relative to a second portion of the catalyst.

9. The method of claim 8 further comprising:

determining an adaptive gain based on the catalyst gain.

10. The method of claim 8 wherein the step of determining a catalyst gain comprises:

estimating the catalyst gain using a polynomial approximation.

11. The method of claim 8 wherein the step of generating the reference air/fuel ratio comprises:

determining a catalyst gain based on signals from the universal exhaust gas oxygen sensor and the heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor;

determining an adaptive gain factor based on the catalyst gain;

determining a difference between a sensor reference voltage and a signal from the heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor;

determining an air/fuel ratio offset based on the difference;
applying the adaptive gain factor to the air/fuel ratio offset to produce an adaptive air/fuel ratio offset;
combining an integral of the difference, the adaptive air/fuel ratio offset, and an air/fuel ratio target value to generate the reference air/fuel ratio signal.

12. The method of claim 11 wherein the step of determining an air/fuel ratio offset comprises determining an air/fuel ratio offset based on the sign of the difference between the reference voltage and the heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor signal.

13. The method of claim 11 wherein the step of determining an air/fuel ratio offset selects one of two offsets based on the difference.

14. A system for controlling a multiple cylinder internal combustion engine, the system comprising:

a first exhaust gas sensor positioned downstream relative to the engine;

a catalyst positioned downstream relative to the first exhaust gas sensor;

a second exhaust gas sensor positioned downstream relative to the first exhaust gas sensor and downstream relative to at least a portion of the catalyst; and

a controller in communication with the first and second exhaust gas sensors, the controller having an inner feedback control loop to control air/fuel ratio of the engine with feedback provided by the first exhaust gas sensor and an outer feedback control loop that modifies a reference air/fuel ratio provided to the inner feedback control loop based on feedback provided by the first and second exhaust gas sensors wherein the reference air/fuel ratio adapts to oxygen storage capacity of the catalyst by estimating a catalyst gain based on the first and second exhaust gas sensors and modifying a reference air/fuel ratio offset based on the catalyst gain.

15. The system of claim 14 wherein the controller determines an adaptive gain based on the catalyst gain and modifies the offset based on the adaptive gain.

16. The system of claim 14 wherein the second exhaust gas sensor is positioned upstream relative to a portion of the catalyst.

17. The system of claim 14 wherein the catalyst comprises a plurality of catalyst elements and wherein the second exhaust gas sensor is positioned downstream relative to a first one of the plurality of catalyst elements.

18. The system of claim 14 wherein the catalyst comprises:

a close-coupled catalyst positioned near an engine exhaust manifold; and

an underbody catalyst positioned downstream relative to the close-coupled catalyst, wherein the second exhaust gas sensor is positioned between the close-coupled catalyst and the underbody catalyst.

19. The system of claim 14 wherein the first exhaust gas sensor provides a signal proportional to oxygen content of exhaust gas upstream of the catalyst.

20. The system of claim 19 wherein the first exhaust gas sensor comprises a universal exhaust gas oxygen sensor.

21. The system of claim 19 wherein the second exhaust gas sensor provides a signal indicative of a relative air/fuel ratio.

22. The system of claim 21 wherein the second exhaust gas sensor comprises a heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor.

23. A computer readable storage medium having stored data representing instructions executable by a computer to control an internal combustion engine having a first exhaust gas oxygen sensor, a catalyst positioned downstream from the first exhaust gas oxygen sensor, and a second exhaust gas oxygen sensor positioned downstream relative to at least a portion of the catalyst, the computer readable storage medium comprising:

instructions for modeling the catalyst as an integrator and determining a catalyst gain based on signals from the first and second exhaust gas oxygen sensors;

instructions for determining an adaptive gain based on the catalyst gain;

instructions for determining a reference air/fuel ratio offset based on a difference between a reference voltage and the second exhaust gas oxygen sensor;

instructions for modifying the air/fuel ratio offset based on the adaptive gain and a target air/fuel ratio to determine a reference air/fuel ratio; and
instructions for generating a fueling command for the engine such that an engine air/fuel ratio approaches the reference air/fuel ratio.

24. The computer readable storage medium of claim 23 wherein the instructions for determining the catalyst gain include instructions for computing a least squares approximation of the catalyst gain.
