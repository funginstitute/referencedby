Lamp for generating high power ultraviolet radiation

 Gary L. Morgan et al

Patent number: 6201355
Filing date: Nov 8, 1999
Issue date: Mar 13, 2001

The apparatus is a gas filled ultraviolet generating lamp for use as a liquid purifier. The lamp is powred by high voltage AC, but has no metallic electrodes within or in contact with the gas enclosure which is constructed as two concentric quartz cylinders sealed together at their ends with the gas fill between the cylinders. Cooling liquid is pumped through the volume inside the inner quartz cylinder where an electrically conductive pipe spaced from the inner cylinder is used to supply the cooling liquid and act as the high voltage electrode. The gas enclosure is enclosed within but spaced from a metal housing which is connected to operate as the ground electrode of the circuit and through which the treated fluid flows. Thus, the electrical circuit is from the central pipe, and through the cooling liquid, the gas enclosure, the treated liquid on the outside of the outer quartz cylinder, and to the housing. Claims

1. A radiation generating lamp comprising:

a housing defining an enclosed volume, said housing including at least one electrically conductive wall;
an electrically conductive member positioned within said enclosed volume; and
a plurality of dielectrics positioned within said enclosed volume between said electrically conductive member and said electrically conductive wall of said housing;
wherein at least one of said plurality of dielectrics comprises a gas capable of generating an energy discharge; and
wherein a second of said plurality of dielectrics comprises a first liquid to be treated with an energy discharge; and
wherein a third of said plurality of dielectrics comprises a second liquid; and
wherein said gas is positioned between said first and second liquids.

2. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 1, wherein said electrically conductive member is electrically connected to a source of high voltage AC energy.

3. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 1, wherein said electrically conductive member is a pipe.

4. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 3, wherein said pipe is in communication with a source of said first or second liquid.

5. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 3, wherein said first or said second liquid enters said defined volume through said pipe.

6. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 5, wherein said first or said second liquid functions as a cooling agent.

7. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 3, wherein said housing further comprises an inlet for said pipe, and said housing further comprises an outlet adapted to permit egress of said first or said second liquid entering said housing through said pipe.

8. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 1, wherein said gas is an excimer gas contained within a gas enclosure.

9. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 8, wherein said gas enclosure is defined by first and second walls, and said first and second walls each comprise one of said plurality of dielectrics.

10. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 9, wherein said first and second walls of said gas enclosure are fabricated from quartz.

11. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 1, wherein said gas is an excimer gas selected from the group consisting of xenon, bromine and mixtures thereof.

12. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 1, wherein said first and second liquids are the same liquid.

13. A radiation generating lamp according to claim 12, wherein said gas is within a gas enclosure and said gas enclosure is electrically isolated from said electrically conductive member and said electrically conductive housing by said first and second liquids, respectively.

14. In an electrical discharge assembly comprising first and second spaced electrodes and a gas enclosure containing a gas capable of generating an electrical discharge positioned between said first and second spaced electrodes, the improvement comprising a first liquid between said first electrode and said gas enclosure, and a second liquid between said gas enclosure and said second electrode.

15. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein said first electrode is a pipe and at least one of said first and second liquids passes through said pipe.

16. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein said second electrode is a wall of a housing and said first electrode, gas enclosure and gas are positioned within said housing.

17. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein said electrical discharge assembly is a radiation generating lamp.

18. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 17, wherein said gas enclosure is defined by one or more dielectric walls fabricated from quartz.

19. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein said first and second liquids have the same composition.

20. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein said gas is an excimer gas.

21. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein at least one of said first and second liquids functions as a dielectric cooling liquid.

22. An electrical discharge assembly according to claim 14, wherein at least one of said first and second liquids is a liquid to be treated with an electrical discharge from said gas.

23. A method for treating a liquid with a radiation generating lamp, comprising:

(a) providing a treatment chamber which comprises first and second electrodes, and a gas enclosure containing a gas capable of generating an electrical discharge positioned between said first and second electrodes;
(b) introducing a first liquid between said first electrode and said gas enclosure, said first liquid functioning at least in part as a dielectric between said first electrode and said gas enclosure; and
(c) introducing a second liquid between said second electrode and said gas enclosure, said second liquid functioning at least in part as a second dielectric between said second electrode and said gas enclosure.

24. A method according to claim 23, wherein said first electrode comprises a pipe and at least one of said first and second liquids is introduced through said pipe.

25. A method according to claim 24, wherein said at least one of said first and second liquids exits said treatment chamber through an outlet.

26. A method according to claim 23, wherein said gas enclosure is defined by first and second walls, and said first and second dielectric walls are fabricated from quartz.

27. A method according to claim 23, wherein at least one of said first and second liquids is a liquid to be treated by an electrical discharge from said gas.

28. A method according to claim 27, wherein said liquid to be treated is introduced to said treatment chamber at a rate which exceeds the rate at which the other liquid is introduced to said treatment chamber.

29. A method according to claim 23, wherein said gas is an excimer gas and said treatment comprises purification of at least one of said first and second liquids.
