In accordance with a specific embodiment of the present invention, an image is projected upon a surface. The image can include a pattern having a plurality of individual shapes used to measure and map the surface. The plurality of individual shapes include features that are detectable in a direction parallel to the plane formed by a projection axis of the projected shapes and a point associated with a view axis. The image further comprises a feature containing an encoding information for identifying the plurality of shapes individually. The feature containing encoding information can be a separate feature from each of the plurality of individual shapes, or may be a feature integral to the plurality of individual shapes. The feature containing encoding information is oriented such that the encoding information is retrieved along a line perpendicular to a plane formed by the projection axis and the point along the view axis. Claims

1. A method of registering data, the method comprising the steps of:

receiving reference independent data representing an object, wherein the reference independent data includes a plurality of frames F1 through Fn, where n is a positive integer greater than 1, and each of the frames F1 through Fn represent a three-dimensional representation of a portion of the object obtained from a scanning the object with a scanner and wherein there is no prior knowledge of the spatial relationship of said frames F1 to Fn to each other due to the data being reference independent; and
performing a registration between frame Fx and frame Fx1, where x is an integer between 1 and n1;
wherein the step of registration further includes the sub-step of: determining a registration entry point between the portions of frame Fx and frame Fx1;
wherein:
each of the plurality of frames is derived from a two-dimensional representation; and wherein the step of determining a registration entry point further includes the step of determining the entry point at least partially based upon a two-dimensional analysis of the said two-dimensional representations of the frames Fx and Fx1.

2. The method of claim 1, wherein the step of determining includes determining an entry point X-coordinate and an entry point Y-coordinate based upon the two-dimensional analysis.

3. The method of claim 2, wherein the two dimensional analysis includes performing a cross-correlation of the scanned two-dimensional representation of frame Fx and Frame Fx1 to determine how many pixels in the X-coordinate have moved, and how many pixels in the Y-coordinate have moved.

4. The method of claim 1, wherein:

the step of determining the registration entry point further includes determining the entry point at least partially based upon a three-dimensional analysis of the frames Fx and Fx1.

5. The method of claim 4, wherein the step of determining includes determining an entry point Z-coordinate based upon the three-dimensional analysis.

6. The method of claim 4, wherein the three-dimensional analysis includes shifting image of the frame Fx1 to a position where the average distance of all the points to the X-coordinate/Y-coordinate plane is zero.

7. A method of registering data, the method comprising the steps of:

receiving a first two-dimensional image representing a first portion of an object, wherein the first two-dimensional image is a reference independent image;
receiving a second two-dimensional image representing a second portion of an object, wherein the second two-dimensional image is a reference independent image;
converting said first and second images into first and second frames, respectively wherein each of said frames comprises a three-dimensional representation of a portion of the object and wherein said first and second images are obtained from a scanning the object with a scanner;
determining an original entry point to be used for performing a three-dimensional registration of the second frame to the first frame;
wherein there is no prior knowledge of the spatial relationship of said frames to each other due to the scanning being reference independent;
performing a three-dimensional registration based on the original entry point;
defining an alternate entry point based upon the original entry point;
performing a three-dimensional registration based on the alternate entry point; and
repeating the steps of defining and performing a three-dimensional registration based on the alternate entry point until all alternate entry points have been tried;
wherein the alternate entry points have a predefined relationship to the original entry point; and
wherein the predefined relationship of the alternate entry points reside in a common plane.
