Battery heat sealing machine and method

 Alan L. Piper et al

Patent number: 4999908
Filing date: Nov 13, 1989
Issue date: Mar 19, 1991

A battery heat seal machine and method for sealing a top cover to a battery container. The machine has a one-piece heating platen with thermal expansion features which eliminate stress on the platen bearings that support the platen. A new type of top cover holder and pickup device is also employed in the machine to positively grasp the top cover around the center line of the cover and to lift it up and return it as desired. The pickup device design may be used with small automotive batteries and with large commercial batteries. A mobile platen transfer device is used with the machine to quickly and safely remove an outgoing platen and replace it with an incoming platen when changing battery sizes and types on the battery assembly line.

Claims

1. In a battery heat sealing machine having a supporting structure and being of the type which normally contains a multi-piece heating platen with a plurality of internal heaters and a thermocouple, with the machine being the type where a battery container, having internal parts and external terminals and having an upper surface and having a top cover and the battery container being heated and sealed together, the improvement comprising:

(a) the platen being formed in a one-piece construction having a central core section and having formed therein a plurality of spaced apart openings being formed in the central core for receipt of the plurality of internal heaters and the thermocouple;
(1) an integral first external heating surface, formed on the central core section, for transferring heat from the heaters and for heating the upper surface of the battery container;
(2) an integral second external heating surface, formed on the central core, for transferring heat from the heaters and for heating the lower surface of the top cover;
(b) mounting means, associated with the one-piece platen and with the heat sealing machine supporting structure for mounting the one-piece platen in the machine; and
(c) the one-piece platen construction with internal spaced apart openings for heaters eliminating warpage in the platen caused by heating and cooling of a multi-piece heating platen and eliminating corrosion in the platen that occurs between adjoining surfaces of a multi-piece heating platen and eliminating heat transfer differential to thereby provide a more uniform heating of the two surfaces on the battery container and on the top cover.

2. The improvement as defined in claim 1 further comprising the one-piece platen containing thermal growth means for thermal expansion of the platen in two directions to relieve stress on the mounting means and the heat sealing machine supporting structure.

3. In a battery heat seal machine having a supporting structure and of the type wherein a battery container and a top cover with vent holes and vent hole sides are moved into place and the top cover is clamped and moved upward from the container and a heating platen with a plurality of bearings is inserted in the machine to cause the upper surface of the battery container and the lower surface of the top cover to be heated by the platen until both surfaces are molten and the container and top cover are disengaged from contact with the heating platen which is then removed to allow the top cover and the battery container to be mated together, the improvement comprising:

(a) the heating platen being formed in a one-piece configuration having two oppositely positioned heating surfaces with a top heating surface for contact with the top cover and with a bottom heating surface for contact with the upper surface of the battery container, the platen containing internal means for heating the two platen surfaces with the one-piece configuration eliminating heat transfer differential and reducing warpage in the platen thereby providing a more uniform heating of the two surfaces to be mated and sealed; and
(b) the one piece heating platen containing thermal expansion means, the expansion means permitting the thermal growth in two lateral directions to remove stress on the bearings of the platen caused by restrained thermal growth and stress on the supporting structure of the machine, the improvements providing a more uniform heating of the upper surface of the battery container and the lower surface of the top cover which are subsequently mated and sealed together.

4. The improvement as defined in claim 3 further comprising the one-piece platen being formed with two pairs of linear bearings mounted on each side of the platen and fixedly attached to opposite sides of the platen, the pairs of oppositely positioned bearings being connected to each other by at least two spring biased rods positioned apart from the platen thereby permitting thermal expansion of the platen in one direction whenever the platen is heated.

5. The improvement as defined in claim 4 further comprising the one-piece platen being movably mounted on the heat seal machine along a pair of elongated rods with the linear bearings being carried by the pair of rods thereby permitting the entire platen to be quickly and easily transferred to an external platen transfer device whenever the platen needs to be changed.

6. The improvement as defined in claim 3 further comprising the machine having positioned thereon a top cover holding device for grasping the top cover and raising and lowering the top cover at predetermined times, the holding device comprising at least two oppositely positioned sidewardly movable and downwardly pointing fingers which may be positioned in the top cover vent holes and may be moved outwardly away from each other to grasp the vent hole sides of the top cover and may be moved inwardly toward each other to release the vent hole sides as desired thereby permitting the holding device to be used to raise and lower the top cover as desired during operation of the machine.

7. The improvement as defined in claim 6 wherein the holding device has at least two pairs of oppositely positioned fingers.

8. The improvement as defined in claim 6 wherein the holding device has at least two groups of three oppositely positioned fingers with each group of three fingers being movable together.

9. The improvement as defined in claim 3 further comprising the one-piece platen being formed with means for quickly releasing the platen from the machine so that the platen may be removed from the machine and another similar platen installed on the machine without lengthy downtime of the machine.

10. A battery heat seal machine for sealing a top cover with a lower surface and vent holes to a battery container having an upper surface comprising:

(a) a frame supporting structure;
(b) a conveyor, fixedly attached to the frame, for moving battery containers and loose top covers into the machine and for moving complete batteries with top covers heat sealed on the battery container away from the machine;
(1) means, associated with the frame and the conveyor, for raising and lowering the battery container at predetermined times;
(c) a one-piece heating platen, movably mounted on the frame and positioned above the conveyor during heating of the platen, the platen having two oppositely positioned heating surfaces with a top heating surface for contact with the top cover and with a bottom heating surface for contact with the upper surface of the battery container, the platen containing internal means for heating the two platen surfaces with the one-piece platen eliminating heat transfer differential in the platen to thereby provide more uniform heating of the top cover and the upper surface of the battery container;
(1) thermal expansion means, associated with the one-piece platen to permit thermal growth of the one-piece platen in two lateral directions to remove stress in the platen bearings and the supporting structure caused by thermal growth of the platen, the one-piece platen providing a more uniform heating of the upper surface of the battery container and the lower surface of the top cover which are subsequently mated and sealed together;
(2) means, associated with the frame and the platen, for moving the platen into and out of its position above the conveyor during heating of the platen;
(d) a top cover holding device for grasping and holding the top cover at predetermined times during operation of the machine;
(1) means, associated with the frame and the holding device, for raising and lowering the holding device and the top cover at predetermined times during operation of the machine; and
(e) means, associated with the machine, for controlling the operation of the various parts of the machine at predetermined times and in a predetermined sequence.

11. The battery heat seal machine as defined in claim 10 wherein the one piece heating platen is formed with linear bearings mounted on each side of the platen and fixedly attached to opposite sides of the platen, the pairs of oppositely positioned bearings being connected to each other by at least two biased rods positioned apart from the platen to permit one direction thermal expansion of the platen whenever the platen is heated.

12. The improvement as defined in claim 10 further comprising at least two of the linear bearings are mounted on an elongated rod with the elongated rod being mounted on a transverse pair of linear bearings and a transverse pair of shorter rods, the mounting on the transverse pair of linear bearings and the transverse pair of shorter rods permitting the platen to thermally expand in a second direction whenever the platen is heated.

13. The battery heat seal machine as defined in claim 10 wherein the top cover holding device is formed with at least two oppositely positioned sidewardly movable and downwardly pointing fingers which may be positioned in the top cover vent holes and may be moved outwardly away from each other to grasp the top cover and may be moved inwardly toward each other to release the top cover as desired thereby permitting the holding device to be used to raise and lower the top cover as desired during operation of the machine.
